##

The barbell bench press is a fundamental upper body compound exercise targeting the pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, and triceps brachii. The lifter lies supine on a flat bench, grasps the barbell with hands approximately 1.5-2× biacromial width, and lowers the bar to the lower sternum before pressing back to full elbow extension. Key technical points: maintain five-point contact (head, shoulders, buttocks, both feet), retract and depress the scapulae throughout, control the eccentric phase over 2-3 seconds, and avoid bouncing the bar off the chest. The optimal bar path is slightly curved — lowering to the lower chest and pressing back toward the upper chest — to reduce shoulder impingement risk.
