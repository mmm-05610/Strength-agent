##

The barbell bent-over row is a fundamental horizontal pulling exercise targeting the latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, trapezius, and biceps. The lifter hinges at the hips with a neutral spine, grasps the bar with an overhand or underhand grip, and pulls the bar to the lower sternum or upper abdomen. Key technique: maintain a hip hinge position with the torso at 45-60 degrees from vertical, keep the bar close to the body throughout, squeeze the scapulae at the top position, and control the lowering phase. The lower back must remain isometrically contracted throughout — this makes the row both a pulling and a core stabilization exercise. Common errors include rounding the back (injury risk), using excessive body momentum (reducing lat involvement), and pulling the bar too high (to the neck/chest rather than abdomen, increasing shoulder stress).
