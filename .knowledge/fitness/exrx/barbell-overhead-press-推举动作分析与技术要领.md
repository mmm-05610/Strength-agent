##

The overhead press (also called the military press or shoulder press) is the primary vertical pressing movement for developing deltoid, triceps, and upper trapezius strength. The lifter stands with feet shoulder-width apart, bar racked at clavicle height, and presses the barbell directly overhead to full elbow extension. Key technique: maintain a rigid trunk by bracing the core and squeezing the glutes, keep the forearms vertical (wrists stacked over elbows), and press the head through at lockout to align the bar over the base of support. The bar path should be as straight as possible with minimal forward deviation.
