##

The lateral raise isolates the medial (side) deltoid, which contributes significantly to shoulder width and the 'capped' shoulder appearance. Key technique: with a slight forward torso lean and soft elbows, raise dumbbells to the sides until arms are approximately parallel to the floor (not higher — beyond this point the upper trapezius takes over). The movement occurs in the scapular plane (approximately 30 degrees forward of pure lateral) to reduce subacromial impingement. Initiate the movement from the elbows (think 'lead with the elbows'), keep the wrists neutral, and control the lowering phase.
