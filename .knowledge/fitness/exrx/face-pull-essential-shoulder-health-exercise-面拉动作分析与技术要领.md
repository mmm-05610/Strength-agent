##

The face pull is a rehabilitative and prehabilitative exercise that targets the rear deltoids, rhomboids, and external rotators of the rotator cuff. It is one of the most recommended exercises by physical therapists for counteracting the negative postural effects of prolonged sitting and excessive pressing. Key technique: using a cable machine with a rope attachment set at approximately face height, grasp the rope with a neutral grip (thumbs pointing back), pull the rope toward the face while externally rotating at the shoulders (palms end up/back), squeeze the scapulae together at the end range, and control the return (don't let the weight stack drop). The final position should have the hands at approximately ear level, elbows flared out and back.
