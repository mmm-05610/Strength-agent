##

The goblet squat is widely considered the best squat teaching tool for beginners. Holding a kettlebell or dumbbell at the chest creates a counterbalance that naturally reinforces an upright torso position, proper depth, and correct knee tracking. Key technique: hold the weight against the chest with both hands (like holding a goblet), feet shoulder-width apart with slight toe-out, initiate the squat by sitting back and down between the heels, keep the weight in contact with the chest throughout, and descend until the elbows touch the inside of the knees (natural depth cue). The goblet position automatically teaches proper bracing (the weight pulls the torso into good alignment) and prevents the common errors of excessive forward lean and heel lifting.
