##

The plank is an isometric anti-extension core exercise that trains the rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, obliques, and spinal erectors to resist lumbar extension. The basic forearm plank: forearms on the ground, elbows under shoulders, body in a straight line from heels to head, glutes and abs engaged. Target: build to 60-second holds before progressing. Progression options: extend the lever arm (arms extended plank), reduce support points (single-leg plank, single-arm plank), add instability (feet on suspension trainer), or add dynamic elements (plank with shoulder taps). The RKC (hard-style) plank variation involves maximal full-body tension — squeeze fists, glutes, and quads while pulling elbows toward toes. Anti-extension training is safer for the spine than flexion-based core exercises (crunches) and more transferable to athletic and daily activities.
