##

The pull-up is a vertical pulling exercise using body weight as resistance. It primarily targets the latissimus dorsi, with secondary involvement of the biceps, rhomboids, and core. Grip variations emphasize different muscle recruitment: pronated (overhand) grip at shoulder-width maximizes lat involvement, supinated (underhand) grip shifts some load to the biceps, wide grip increases lat emphasis but reduces range of motion and increases shoulder stress. Key technique: initiate the pull by depressing the scapulae (not just bending the elbows), drive the elbows down and back, pull until the chin clears the bar (or chest touches for higher ROM), and control the lowering phase over 2-3 seconds.
