## NSCA Program Design Principles

The National Strength and Conditioning Association (NSCA) outlines evidence-based principles for designing resistance training programs for novice athletes and general fitness clients.

## Needs Analysis

Before writing any program, conduct a needs analysis addressing:
**Movement requirements**: What muscle groups and movement patterns are involved in the client's goals or sport?
**Physiological requirements**: Strength, power, hypertrophy, or muscular endurance?
**Injury profile**: Previous injuries, current limitations, movement compensations.
**Training availability**: Days per week, session duration, equipment access.
**Training age**: Novice (<6 months consistent training), Intermediate (6-24 months),

## Rest Period Recommendations

- Structural/core exercises: 2-5 minutes
- Assistance exercises: 1-2 minutes
- Novices may need slightly longer rest (3-4 min) while learning technique
- Rest intervals are based on the goal: strength/power = longer rest, endurance = shorter rest
