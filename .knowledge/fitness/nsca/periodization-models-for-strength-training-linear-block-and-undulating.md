## Periodization Models

## Periodization Models

Periodization is the systematic planning of training variables over time to optimize performance and avoid overtraining. The NSCA recognizes three primary periodization models.

## Linear Periodization

The most traditional model. Begins with high volume and low intensity, gradually transitioning to low volume and high intensity over a macrocycle (typically 12-16 weeks).
**Example for novice**:

- Weeks 1-4: 3 sets × 10-12 RM (hypertrophy emphasis)
- Weeks 5-8: 4 sets × 8-10 RM (strength-hypertrophy)
- Weeks 9-12: 4-5 sets × 4-6 RM (maximal strength)

## Undulating (Non-Linear) Periodization

## Undulating (Non-Linear) Periodization

Training variables (volume, intensity) fluctuate more frequently — daily or weekly.
**Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP)** example week:

- Monday: 3×4 RM (strength emphasis)
- Wednesday: 3×12 RM (hypertrophy emphasis)
- Friday: 3×8 RM (strength-hypertrophy blend)
  Research suggests DUP may produce slightly greater strength gains than linear periodization in trained individuals, but differences are small. For novices, any structured approach produces significant gains.

## Key Principle

## Key Principle

The optimal periodization model depends on training age, goals, schedule, and recovery capacity. Novices benefit most from simple linear models. Consistency in applying the chosen model matters far more than the choice of model itself.
