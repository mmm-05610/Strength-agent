## Abstract

## Abstract

Meta-regression of 38 resistance training studies (n=1,823 participants) examining the relationship between weekly set volume per muscle group and hypertrophic outcomes. A clear dose-response relationship emerged: <5 weekly sets (maintenance/minimal effect, ES=0.21), 5-9 sets (small effect, ES=0.35), 10-19 sets (moderate effect, ES=0.55), 20-29 sets (large effect, ES=0.72). Beyond ~30 sets per muscle group per week, marginal gains diminished while indicators of overreaching (elevated CK, decreased performance) increased. Each additional set beyond 10 weekly sets contributed approximately 2-3% additional hypertrophy, suggesting a point of diminishing returns. Not all muscle groups responded equally: biceps and triceps reached near-maximal gains at lower volumes (~15 sets/week)
