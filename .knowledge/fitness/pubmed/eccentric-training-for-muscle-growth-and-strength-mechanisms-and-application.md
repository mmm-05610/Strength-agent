## Abstract

Review of eccentric (lengthening) contraction training and its unique role in resistance exercise. Eccentric actions produce 1.3-1.5× greater force than concentric actions at the same relative load, resulting in greater mechanical tension per motor unit. Eccentric training preferentially recruits fast-twitch (Type II) muscle fibers even at relatively low loads, providing a potent hypertrophic stimulus. Eccentric-emphasized training (3-5 second lowering phase) increased muscle protein synthesis rates by 20-30% above concentric-only training in acute studies. Practical applications for novices: moderate eccentric tempos (2-3 seconds) improve motor learning and technique mastery.
