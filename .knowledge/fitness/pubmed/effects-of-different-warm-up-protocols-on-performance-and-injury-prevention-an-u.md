## Abstract

Review of 32 studies on warm-up efficacy. Dynamic warm-ups improved subsequent power output by 3-7% compared to no warm-up. Static stretching alone (without subsequent dynamic activity) reduced maximal strength by 3-5% and power by 2-8% when stretches were held for >60 seconds. Combining static stretching with subsequent dynamic movements mitigated these performance decrements. Structured warm-up programs (including FIFA 11+) reduced overall injury risk by 30-50% in team sports when implemented consistently. The physiological mechanisms include: increased muscle compliance at elevated temperatures, enhanced neural conduction velocity, improved joint lubrication via synovial fluid distribution, and psychological preparation (increased focus and arousal).
