## Abstract

This meta-analysis of 28 randomized controlled trials (n=1,247 participants) examined the dose-response relationship between progressive overload implementation and strength/hypertrophy outcomes. Results indicated that structured progressive overload (2.5-5% load increase per week) produced significantly greater strength gains (ES=0.76, 95% CI: 0.58-0.94, p<0.001) compared to non-progressive protocols. Hypertrophy differences were smaller but significant (ES=0.38, 95% CI: 0.19-0.57, p=0.002). The '2-for-2 rule' (increase load when 2 additional reps can be performed in 2 consecutive sessions) was the most studied progression method and showed high adherence (87%). Double progression (first increase reps within a range, then increase load) demonstrated the best balance of progression rate and injury avoidance.
