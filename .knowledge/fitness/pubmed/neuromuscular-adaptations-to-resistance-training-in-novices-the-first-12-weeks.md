## Abstract

## Abstract

This 12-week study tracked neuromuscular adaptations in 45 novice trainees (22 male, 23 female, mean age 24.6 years) using surface EMG, interpolated twitch technique, and MRI. Results revealed a two-phase adaptation pattern: Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Early strength gains (15-20%) occurred without significant muscle hypertrophy (<2% CSA increase), driven primarily by neural adaptations — increased motor unit recruitment (EMG amplitude +28%), improved intermuscular coordination, and reduced antagonist co-activation. Phase 2 (Weeks 5-12): Hypertrophy became the dominant contributor (CSA +8.2% by week 12), with neural adaptations plateauing.
