## Abstract

This critical review re-examines the evidence for the post-exercise 'anabolic window' concept. While early research suggested a narrow 30-60 minute window for protein ingestion post-exercise, more recent evidence indicates the window is wider than previously thought, especially when pre-exercise protein has been consumed. The total daily protein intake and per-meal protein distribution (0.4-0.55 g/kg per meal across 4-5 meals) are more important determinants of long-term muscle protein balance than precise post-exercise timing. However, post-exercise protein remains important for three scenarios: (1) when training in a fasted state, (2) when the pre-exercise meal was consumed >4-6 hours prior, (3) during periods of high training volume or when training twice daily.
