## Abstract

Systematic review of 19 studies examining rest interval (RI) effects on resistance training outcomes. For maximal strength development, longer RIs (3-5 minutes) consistently outperformed shorter RIs (1-2 minutes), with pooled ES=0.55 (95% CI: 0.32-0.78) favoring longer rest. For hypertrophy, moderate RIs (1-2 minutes) appeared sufficient, though evidence was mixed with some studies showing no significant difference between 1, 2, and 3-minute RIs. Short RIs (30-60 seconds) produced greater acute hormonal responses (GH, testosterone) but did not translate to greater chronic hypertrophic adaptations. For muscular endurance, short RIs (<60 seconds) provided a specific training stimulus.
