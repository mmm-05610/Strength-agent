## Abstract

This review analyzed 18 studies comparing different repetition tempos. Key findings: (1) Repetition durations from 2-8 seconds produced similar hypertrophy when sets were taken to or near volitional failure, suggesting total volume load matters more than tempo per se. (2) Very slow tempos (10+ seconds per rep) required significant load reductions and produced inferior strength gains compared to moderate tempos. (3) Intentionally fast concentric phases (1-2 seconds) maximized power output and rate of force development. (4) Controlled eccentric phases (2-3 seconds) improved technique, reduced injury risk, and ensured consistent muscle tension. (5) For novices, a 2-1-2 tempo (2s eccentric, 1s pause, 2s concentric) provides an optimal balance of motor learning, safety, and stimulus.
