## Abstract

## Abstract

This systematic review examined 45 studies investigating optimal resistance training (RT) prescription for novice trainees. The analysis focused on four key training variables: frequency, volume, intensity, and progression models.

## Objective

To synthesize current evidence on resistance training variables that maximize strength and hypertrophic

## Methods

A systematic search of PubMed, Scopus, and SPORTDiscus databases (2015-2025) yielded 45 studies meeting inclusion criteria: (1) participants with <6 months RT experience, (2) intervention duration ≥8 weeks, (3) reported strength and/or hypertrophy outcomes, (4) randomized controlled trials or controlled trials.

## Results

## Results

**Training Frequency**

- 2 sessions/week produced significant strength gains (effect size [ES]=0.82, 95% CI: 0.65-0.99)
- 3 sessions/week showed marginally greater gains (ES=0.91, 95% CI: 0.72-1.10), difference not statistically significant (p=0.23)
- Per-muscle frequency of 2x/week superior to 1x/week (ES=0.45, p<0.01)

## Conclusions

## Conclusions

Novice trainees achieve optimal results with 2-3 weekly sessions, 10-20 sets per muscle group per week, at 60-80% 1RM, using linear or double progression. Technical proficiency should be prioritized over lo

## Practical Applications

1. Beginners should start with 2 sessions/week for the first 4-8 weeks
2. Focus on 4-6 compound exercises per session, 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
3. Prioritize technique mastery before increasing lo
